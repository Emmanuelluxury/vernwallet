Learn About Bitcoin-Starknet Bridge

Everything you need to know about bridging Bitcoin to Starknet, how it works, and why it matters for the future of decentralized finance.

What is Bitcoin?

Bitcoin is the first decentralized cryptocurrency, launched in 2009. It operates on a peer-to-peer network without central authority, using proof-of-work consensus to secure transactions recorded on its blockchain.

Bitcoin provides a store of value and medium of exchange with a fixed supply of 21 million coins, making it resistant to inflation.

What is Starknet?

Starknet is a decentralized Validity-Rollup (also known as a "ZK-Rollup") that operates as an L2 network over Ethereum. It enables scalable decentralized applications without compromising on security.

Using STARK proofs, Starknet batches transactions off-chain and submits cryptographic proofs to Ethereum, dramatically reducing costs while maintaining security.

Why Bridge Bitcoin to Starknet?

Bridging Bitcoin to Starknet unlocks Bitcoin's liquidity for DeFi applications while maintaining Bitcoin's security. This allows Bitcoin holders to:

  • Participate in DeFi protocols
  • Trade with low fees and fast transactions
  • Access innovative financial products
  • Maintain exposure to Bitcoin's value

How the Bridge Works

1

Lock Bitcoin

Users send Bitcoin to a secure multisignature address on the Bitcoin blockchain, which locks the Bitcoin.

2

Generate Proof

The bridge generates a cryptographic proof verifying the Bitcoin transaction and the amount locked.

3

Mint wrapped BTC

Based on the proof, an equivalent amount of wrapped Bitcoin (wBTC) is minted on Starknet.

4

Use on Starknet

Users can now use their wBTC in Starknet's DeFi ecosystem with low fees and fast transactions.

5

Reverse Process

To redeem Bitcoin, users burn wBTC on Starknet and provide proof to unlock the original Bitcoin.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the bridge trustless?
The bridge uses a combination of cryptographic proofs and economic incentives to minimize trust requirements. While not fully trustless, it significantly reduces trust assumptions compared to traditional bridges.
What are the fees for using the bridge?
Bridge fees consist of: Bitcoin network fees for the lock/unlock transactions, a small bridge service fee (0.1-0.2%), and Starknet gas fees for minting/burning wBTC. Total fees typically range from 0.3% to 0.5% of the transferred amount.
How long does a transfer take?
Bitcoin to Starknet transfers typically take 30-60 minutes (1 Bitcoin confirmation + proof generation time). Starknet to Bitcoin transfers take 2-4 hours due to additional security waiting periods.
Is there a minimum transfer amount?
Yes, the minimum transfer amount is 0.001 BTC to ensure economic viability given network fees. There's no maximum limit, but large transfers may require additional security checks.
What are the risks?
Potential risks include: smart contract vulnerabilities, bridge operator malfeasance, Bitcoin network reorganization, and Starknet protocol risks. The bridge implements multiple security measures to mitigate these risks.
